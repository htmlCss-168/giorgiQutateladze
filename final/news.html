Scream VI 4K and Blu-ray Release Date, Special Features

Fans will soon be able to buy Scream VI in 4K, Blu-ray, DVD, and digital as the movie is approaching its domestic release. Starting on July 11, Scream VI will arrive on 4K, Blu-ray, DVD, and a special 4K Steelbook edition. The movie debuted in theaters on March 10 and grossed $167 million at the box office. It marked the third-highest result in the franchise history behind 1996’s Scream ($173 million) and Scream 2 ($172.4 million). Scream VI is also coming to the digital market both as a standalone and as part of a special bundle featuring 2022’s Scream starting April 25.

Star-Lord Swears at Nebula in Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 Clip

A new Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 clip from the highly anticipated upcoming Marvel movie has been released ahead of the film’s release date on May 5, 2023. The 45-second Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 clip begins with the Guardians rushing out of a house and Star-Lord (Chris Pratt) telling the Drax (Dave Bautista) to stay behind and watch Rocket Racoon (Bradley Cooper). He then tells Mantis (Pom Klementieff) to watch after Drax after the former begins complaining. After handing Groot (Vin Diesel) some weapons, Star-Lord then has to instruct Nebula (Karen Gillan) how to get into the car, which results in Star-Lord swearing at her.

Number of GOTG Vol. 3 Post-Credit Scenes Revealed by James Gunn

The Marvel Cinematic Universe is no stranger to dramatic end sequences that leave fans guessing what will come next, and according to James Gunn, there will be several Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 post-credit scenes. Replying to a recent fan’s question on Instagram, Gunn revealed that there would be not one but two post-credit scenes in the upcoming superhero movie. While this might not as a shock due to how most Marvel Studios’ movies feature two post-credit scenes, fans will be happy to know that the Vol. 3 post-credit scenes follow the formula.

James Gunn Debunks Vin Diesel Guardians of the Galaxy Pay Rumor

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 director James Gunn has dispelled a rumor about how much series star Vin Diesel was paid for each movie. On his Twitter account, Gunn responded to a rumor that claimed that Diesel, who plays the alien tree person and Guardians of the Galaxy member Groot, made $13 million for each movie that he voiced the character in. The director replied with a laughing emoji, followed by a simple “not true.”
